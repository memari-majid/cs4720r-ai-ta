ASSIGNMENTS (with full details):

Week 00 Discussion — "Introduce Yourself" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-08-29
Welcome to CS 4720R! Introduce yourself to the class. Share: your name, your major, what excites you about AI in education, and one problem in education (K-12 or higher ed) you wish technology could solve. Video or text — your choice!

Week 01 Discussion — "EdTech Products That Run on AI" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-09-05
Find one real EdTech product or startup that uses AI to improve teaching or learning. Share the link and explain: what does it do, who is the target user (teacher, student, admin, parent?), and would you pay for it? Examples to explore: tutoring bots, grading assistants, lesson planners, accessibility tools.

Agent Build 1: Workflow Automation | 50.0 pts | due: 2026-09-12


Week 02 Discussion — "The Most Tedious Task in Education" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-09-12
Talk to a teacher, professor, or school administrator (or reflect on your own experience). What is the most tedious, repetitive task they deal with? Describe how you would automate it with n8n. What would it cost a school to have a person do this manually every week?

Agent Build 2: Text AI Agent | 50.0 pts | due: 2026-09-19


Week 03 Discussion — "Your First AI Teaching Assistant" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-09-19
Your text AI agent costs $10-30/mo. A human tutor or teaching assistant costs $15-25/hr. Write a 3-sentence pitch to a school principal or department chair explaining why they should "hire" your AI tutoring agent to support students after school hours.

Agent Build 3: Content Pipeline | 50.0 pts | due: 2026-09-26


Week 04 Discussion — "AI-Generated Lesson Plans & Content" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-09-26
A teacher spends 5-10 hours/week creating lesson plans, worksheets, and assessments. Your content agent can generate a first draft in minutes. But is the quality good enough? What does the AI do well for education content? Where is a human educator still essential?

Agent Build 4: Knowledge & Data Agent (RAG) | 50.0 pts | due: 2026-10-03


Week 05 Discussion — "RAG: When AI Needs to Know Your Curriculum" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-10-03
You built a RAG agent this week. Imagine feeding it a school's entire curriculum guide, textbook, or student handbook. Compare: ask your agent a question WITH those documents vs WITHOUT. Share both answers. How much better is the RAG version? What education scenarios need this most?

Agent Build 5: Voice Agent | 50.0 pts | due: 2026-10-10


Week 06 Discussion — "Voice AI in Education" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-10-10
Imagine a voice AI agent that helps students practice a foreign language, reads aloud to struggling readers, or provides accessibility support for students with disabilities. A human aide costs $25-35K/yr. Your voice agent works 24/7 for $50-200/mo. What are the benefits for students? What are the ethical concerns of replacing human interaction in education?

Assignment 1: Five Agent Portfolio | 150.0 pts | due: 2026-10-17


Week 07 Discussion — "Your EdTech AI Team" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-10-17
Share your EdTech company org chart where each role is one of your five agents (A through E). How would they work together to serve a school or district? For example: Agent A handles enrollment forms, Agent B tutors students, Agent C creates worksheets, Agent D answers policy questions from a handbook (RAG), Agent E calls parents. Which agent would be most valuable to a real school — and why?

Week 08 Discussion — "When Education AI Goes Wrong" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-10-24
Share the worst failure you've seen from one of your agents. What did it do wrong? How did you fix it? Now think about the stakes in education: what's the scariest thing an AI agent could do if left unchecked in a school (wrong grade, inappropriate content, biased feedback)? How do we build guardrails for student safety?

Production-Ready Agent System | 100.0 pts | due: 2026-10-31


Week 09 Discussion — "Production-Ready: What Changed?" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-10-31
Compare your agent system now vs. Week 7. What did you refactor? What breaks less? Share one specific improvement that made your system more reliable. How would a school IT administrator feel about deploying your current system vs. your Week 7 version?

Week 10 Discussion — "Your EdTech Lean Canvas: The Problem" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-11-07
Share your Lean Canvas Problem box: what are the top 3 problems your target education customer faces? Who is that customer specifically — a 3rd-grade teacher, a high school counselor, a university registrar? Be as concrete as possible. Prof. Ruggles and Prof. Memari will provide feedback.

Startup Package: Lean Canvas + Interviews + MVP | 200.0 pts | due: 2026-11-14


Week 11 Discussion — "What Educators Actually Want" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-11-14
What was the most surprising thing you heard in your customer interviews with teachers, students, or administrators? Did it change your EdTech idea? Share one assumption about education that was proven wrong — or confirmed.

Week 12 Discussion — "Unit Economics: Is Your EdTech Viable?" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-11-19
Calculate: what does your AI team cost per month? What would human staff cost for the same roles in a school? Share the comparison. At what price point does your EdTech product become affordable for a school or district budget?

Week 13 Discussion — "Practice Pitch & Peer Feedback" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-11-23
Post your 3-5 minute practice pitch video for your EdTech product. Then watch at least 2 classmates' pitches and reply with constructive feedback. Would a school principal buy this? What was strong? What could improve?

Week 14 Discussion — "What Are You Fixing?" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-12-05
After your dress rehearsal and mentor session with Prof. Ruggles and Prof. Memari, what is the ONE thing you most need to improve before Demo Day? Reply to one classmate with a concrete suggestion.

Business Report: Unit Economics and Architecture | 100.0 pts | due: 2026-12-12


Demo Day: Pitch + Live Demo | 200.0 pts | due: 2026-12-12


Week 15 Discussion — "Course Takeaways" | 5.0 pts | due: 2026-12-12
What was the most valuable thing you learned this semester about building AI-powered EdTech products? How will you use these skills in your career? Share your biggest takeaway with the class.

## Weekly Rhythm

Each week students complete:

1. **Explore** — Read/watch assigned materials about the week's EdTech problem
2. **Discuss** — Post a reflection connecting the topic to real education challenges
3. **Build** — Create a working prototype in n8n during class (lab)
4. **Reflect** — How does this prototype solve a real problem for educators or students?
